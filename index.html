Challenges

  1. The right team.
  2. The right data.
  3. The right standards.
  4. The right infrastructure.

The right team is balanced

  • Into civic tech and open data, but not establishment.
    Interaction design study group in 2009.
  • Programming + politics.
    Poplus based PMO in 2014.
  • Community driven vs Donor driven.
    Election crowdsourcing failure in 2015.

The right data is interoprable

  • No data.
    Crowdsourcing is harder than scraping for the environmental impact map in 2009.
  • Not open.
    Argentina's FOIA law in 2016
  • No identifiers.
    Can't cross refrence company owners in 2017 sousveillance hackathon.
  • No standards.
    Translating from Excel to OCDS old mexican contracting data in 2018.

The right standards are flexible, expressive and updated

  1. Popolo Project vs Akoma Ntoso standard.
    Poplus federation helped add Events in Popolo to express voting in 2015.
  2. One OCDS does not fit all.
    Is the Open Contracting Data Standard too hard or too easy?
  3. New Open Ownership standard.
    Should it be stand alone or an extension?

The right infrastructure is free and free

  • Community built reusable software.
    RIP the Poplus Federation circa 2013-2016.
  • Community hosted APIs and databases.
    PopIt was discontinued in 2016, continued by Cargografias and Sinar.
  • Free commercial cloud vs Government hosted vs Civic cloud.
    Maintaining just a few servers is hard and expensive.
