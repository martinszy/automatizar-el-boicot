Challenges

  1. The right team.
  2. The right data.
  3. The right standards.
  4. The right infrastructure.

The right team

  • Is into civic tech and open data (Study group in 2009)
  • Right mix between programming and politics (Poplus based PMO in 2014).
  • Community driven vs Donor driven (Election crowdsourcing failure in 2015).

The right data

  • No data (crowdsourcing is harder than scraping for the environmental impact map in 2009).
  • Not open (Argentina's FOIA law in 2016)
  • No identifiers (Can't cross refrence company owners in 2017 sousveillance hackathon)
  • No standards (Translating from Excel to OCDS old mexican contracting data in 2018).

The right standards

  1. Popolo Project vs African parliament standard (added Events to express voting in 2014).
  2. Open Contracting Data Standard (is it too hard or too easy?)
  3. Open Ownership (stand alone or extension?).

The right infrastructure

  • Community built reusable software (RIP the Poplus Federation circa 2013-2016).
  • Community hosted APIs and databases (PopIt was discontinued in 2016).
  • Free commercial cloud vs Government hosted vs Civic cloud.
